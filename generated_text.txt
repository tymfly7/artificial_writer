China, officially known as the People's Republic of China (PRC), is located in East Asia and has a population of over 1.4 billion, making it the second-most populous country in the world after India, accounting for 17.4% of the global population. The country covers nearly 9.6 million square kilometers, ranking as the third-largest by total land area, and shares borders with fourteen countries. It is administratively divided into 33 province-level divisions, which include 22 provinces, five autonomous regions, four municipalities, and two semi-autonomous special administrative regions. Beijing serves as the capital, while Shanghai is the most populous city and the largest financial center.

Historically, China is recognized as one of the cradles of civilization, with human habitation dating back to the Paleolithic era. By the late 2nd millennium BCE, the first dynastic states emerged in the Yellow River basin. The period from the 8th to the 3rd centuries BCE was marked by the decline of the Zhou dynasty, leading to advancements in administration, military techniques, literature, philosophy, and historiography. In 221 BCE, China was unified under an emperor, initiating over two thousand years of imperial rule characterized by notable dynasties such as the Qin, Han, Tang, Yuan, Ming, and Qing. Significant cultural achievements during this time included the invention of gunpowder and paper, the establishment of the Silk Road, and the construction of the Great Wall, all of which have had a lasting impact on both Chinese culture and the world.
